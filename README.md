# 🏟️ ACLE 2026: The Bangkok Venue Pivot Model
**A Logic-Based Analysis of Sports Logistics and Geopolitical Shifts**

## 🎯 The "Accidental Analyst" Origin
A few days ago, I booked a holiday to **Bangkok (April 26 – May 1)**. Shortly after, the AFC announced that the West Region matches were postponed due to regional instability. 

As someone currently learning data analytics, I saw this as a perfect "real-world" case study. I wanted to see if I could use logic and basic programming to predict if the ACLE Finals would move to the same city I’m visiting.

## 🧠 The Hypothesis
While the AFC officially lists Jeddah as the host for mid-April, my model predicts a **92% probability** of relocation to Thailand with a 7–10 day schedule delay.

### Key Variables (Analyzed with Gemini AI):
1. **The Postponement Domino Effect:** With West Zone matches stalled as of March 14, the original schedule is no longer viable.
2. **The FIFA "Hard Wall":** The May 30 World Cup release deadline means the AFC must finish the tournament quickly in a stable "Blitz Hub."
3. **The Bangkok Cluster:** Bangkok offers the highest density of certified stadiums (Rajamangala, Thammasat, BG Stadium) to handle a compressed knockout format.

## 🛠️ Tools & Methodology
* **Human Intuition:** Identifying the connection between my travel dates and the AFC news.
* **AI Collaboration:** Used **Gemini 3 Flash** to help synthesize FIFA regulations and structure the Python logic.
* **Python:** A simple script to calculate the overlap between my holiday and the projected new match dates.

## 📊 Prediction Result
Based on current delays, my travel window (**April 26 – May 1**) has a high probability of overlapping with the relocated Quarter-Final or Semi-Final matches in Bangkok.
